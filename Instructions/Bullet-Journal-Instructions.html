BoJo — The bullet journal

The bullet journal (BoJo) is a method developed by Ryder Carroll — a digital product designer.

According to the creator of this method, the BoJo method helps you:

  • Track the past,
  • Organize the present, and
  • Plan for the future.

    • With the bullet journal, you can organize your:

  • Daily to-do lists,
  • Weekly or monthly calendars, and
  • Track your short-term and long-term goals.

    • Time management skills it improves:

  • Effective planning
  • Stress management
  • SMART goal setting

    • Issues it solves:

  • Missed deadlines
  • Ineffective scheduling

    • At the end of each month, you should review your tasks.

    Advantages of the Bullet Journal

  • The BoJo method helps you plan your long-term goals (for the next six months) and align them with your short-term goals (your weekly and daily goals).

    • Disadvantages of the Bullet Journal

  • This technique can be too time-consuming — to make an effective BoJo journal from scratch, you’ll need to invest time, so it won’t be quite practical if you’re a busy person.
